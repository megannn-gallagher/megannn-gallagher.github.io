Contributor Code of Conduct

We have a few basic rules you must follow when contributing to this site. These rules are in place to ensure we remain a reliable source for information on company ethics, and to reduce the spread of misinformation.

Any breach of this policy, or any other policy, will lead to action being taken including suspension from contributing, and bans.

Any questions about this policy should be sent to codeofconduct-questions@example.com.

The rules

  1. All contributions to this website must comply with this policy, the Reliable Information Policy, and any other policy which we publish.
  2. Be civil, but feel free to debate on the discussion pages.
  3. You must add any conflicts of interest, or anything which could be perceived as a conflict of interest, to your profile.
    An example of this is working for a company which has a profile on this website. Only our independent auditors can see this.
  4. You must not, under any circumstances, attempt to (positively or negatively) influence the public perception of a company through this site for your personal gain.
  5. You must not publish any new information on this site. This site is designed to summarise publicly available information about companies; whistleblowers are not allowed.
